See

Jerónimos Monastery A stunning example of Manueline architecture. Built in the 16th century to commemorate Vasco da Gama's voyage to India. Entry is free on Sundays before 2 pm.
Belém Tower A fortified tower built in the early 16th century as a ceremonial gateway to Lisbon. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Expect queues in peak season.
São Jorge Castle Medieval castle overlooking the city. Walk the battlements for panoramic views of the Tagus and Alfama district. Geocaching enthusiasts will find a cache here. 38.7139; -9.1334

Do

Tram 28 Ride The iconic yellow tram winds through Alfama, Graça, and Estrela. Ride early morning to avoid tourist crush. Buy tickets onboard or use a Viva Viagem card.
LX Factory A repurposed industrial complex in Alcântara packed with independent shops, restaurants, and a popular Sunday market. Live music on weekends.

Eat

Pastéis de Belém The original pastel de nata bakery, operating since 1837. The recipe is a closely guarded secret. Arrive early — queues form quickly.
Taberna da Rua das Flores Tiny traditional taberna serving petiscos (Portuguese tapas). No reservations — arrive before opening to queue. Wine list focuses on natural Portuguese producers.

Drink

Park Bar Rooftop bar on the top floor of a car park in Bairro Alto. Stunning sunset views. Minimal signage — enter through the car park entrance on Calçada do Combro.
Pensão Amor A former brothel turned bar and bookshop in Cais do Sodré's Pink Street. Burlesque decor, resident DJ, and a curated selection of erotic literature.
